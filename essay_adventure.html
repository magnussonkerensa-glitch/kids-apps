The Essay Sandwich 🥪

Every essay ever written has these same three layers. Yes, really.

🍞
Top slice

The Introduction

Hook the reader with a surprising fact, a bold question, or a vivid image. Then tell them what you're going to argue. Like a movie trailer — make them want to keep reading.

🥬
Filling

Main Body — Point 1

Your strongest argument. State it clearly, back it up with evidence or an example, then explain why it matters. One idea per paragraph.

🍅
More filling

Main Body — Point 2 (and 3)

Your next best point. Same recipe: state it, prove it, explain it. Aim for 2–3 body paragraphs for a school essay.

🍞
Bottom slice

The Conclusion

Bring it home. Briefly remind the reader of your main points and land your big final thought. Don't introduce new ideas — just stick the landing.

💡 The Golden Rule: Tell them what you'll say → say it → tell them what you said. That's every essay, ever.
